My name is Edward Shim, and I am currently a senior at the University of Pennsylvania majoring in Cognitive Science with a concentration in Computation & Cognition (College of Arts and Sciences) and minoring in Consumer Psychology (CAS and Wharton School of Business). Through my major and minor, I have obtained quantitative and qualitative skills.

I am passionate about learning the relationship among human cognitive abilities, computational abilities, and consumer behaviors. Due to the interdisciplinary nature of my major, I have taken courses in computer science, psychology, marketing, statistics, and linguistics to understand the connections more thoroughly. In high school, I have also written and published research projects titled "Game Theory Analysis of the Proof of Work Consensus Algorithm" and "A Multiple Linear Regression Analysis of Virginia Schools to Predict Dropout Rates" to dig deeper into the art of cryptography and artificial intelligence. Since high school, my passion has shifted toward analyzing the art of computation and the human mind, which I hope to apply in the financial, consulting, and entrepreneurship industries.

The art of dribbling, passing, and shooting of these two players has entranced me since I was a child. I have also played and captained my varsity lacrosse team in high school, in which my footwork from soccer came in handy. Cooking quickly became my hobby in high school, as I love food. I have taught myself how to cook multiple cuisines by watching YouTube videos and my mother's cooking. I would cook for my family, friends, and myself whenever possible. I learned to cook cuisines like Korean, Japanese, Chinese, English, French, Italian, American, and more through the basic cooking skills I have obtained over time. When I am not doing any work, you can find me doing something related to soccer, poker, or cooking.
